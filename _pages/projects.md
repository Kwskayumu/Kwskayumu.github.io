## Angles-Only Initial Relative Orbit Determination for Small-Body Proximity Operations
*Research · Astrodynamics and Space Systems Laboratory · Apr. 2025 – present*

Developing an angles-only initial relative orbit determination algorithm for a non-cooperative
ultra-compact sampling probe in small-body proximity operations. The method exploits nonlinear
dynamics to resolve the depth ambiguity inherent to bearings-only measurements, and is validated
through Monte Carlo simulations in a high-fidelity environment with polyhedral gravity, SRP, and
realistic sensor noise.

## Swarm-Robot Exploration of Unknown Environments Using Lévy Walk
*Research · Nishinari/Yanagisawa Laboratory · Apr. 2024 – Mar. 2025*

Applied the Lévy walk stochastic exploration strategy to swarm-robot search of unknown planetary
surface environments, quantitatively evaluating search efficiency across varying obstacle and
resource distributions.

## HELIOS-R — C&DH subsystem
*ISAS/JAXA on-orbit technology demonstration project · Apr. 2025 – present*

Contributing to the C&DH (Command & Data Handling) subsystem for
[HELIOS-R](https://assl.isas.jaxa.jp/projects/helios-r/helios-r.html), a lightweight deployable
membrane structure with integrated power-generation and antenna functions (a 1 m-per-side
triangular woven membrane), developed under ISAS/JAXA laboratory leadership with student members as
core contributors. HELIOS-R was launched as a payload aboard JAXA's RAISE-4 small demonstration
satellite on Rocket Lab's Electron rocket (FY2025).

## FUNADE — CubeSat Flight Software
*The University of Tokyo CubeSat development project · Jun. 2024 – present*

Contributing to flight software development for [FUNADE](https://www.furukawaelectric.com/product/aerospace/funade/),

## Drone-Type CanSat
*The University of Tokyo student competition project · Apr. – Sep. 2023*

As Sub-PM, coordinated an 11-person team developing a novel drone-type CanSat targeting a 0-meter
landing goal, and implemented drone flight control with Pixhawk and MAV-SDK. Competed at the 2023
Noshiro Space Event and ARLISS 2023.
